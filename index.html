About

A public noticed to renew the prospecting license was published on the 25th July 2019. The public have 30 days to make any complaints.

BTU Metals Corp. purchased the prospecting rights to a 75 square km area in May of last year. The initial licence was granted in 2013 and the company are now planning on acting on prospecting carried out in the area in 2015.

This area runs from Leenane, through Maam Valley as far as Corr na Móna and consists of a number of already prospected sites. From the map provided on the BTU website there are 10 already prospected sites and according to an article published in Galwaydaily.com they are already preparing and have funding in place for commencing works on the Lee site.

BTU Company Website

From the Department of Communications, Climate Action and Environment website the remaining PL areas on the map were submitted in the prospecting licence competition this May, meaning if the licences are granted BTU will have the prospecting rights to the entire area and are a step closer to potentially mining the entire area.

Public Notice to renew prospecting license in Maam Valley

Needless to say any form of mining would have detrimental effects on the flora fauna and aqua life in the area as well as the greater area of Lough Corrib. The use of ARESNIC and CYANIDE are commonplace in the mining process and the potential for this to seep into our land and waterways would cause irreversible damage.

All letters of objection should be made to the Exploration and Mining Division of the DCCAE at the following email address:
